The Federal Emergency Management Agency’s new online tool shows that 53 percent of the city’s properties (in green) are no longer in a so-called high-risk zone and do not need to carry flood insurance. Areas in gray have no change in their flood zone. Areas in red (roughly 5 percent) are now in a “higher risk” zone. FEMA insists the maps are insurance rating tools and should not be interpreted as life and safety flood maps.
Screenshot from FEMA RiskMap website
Intermap Technologies creates flood maps for private insurance companies to calculate premium costs and payout amounts. Its maps show the entire New Orleans metro area as “high” or “very high” (dark purple) flood risk (a 5 percent annual chance of flooding). Unlike FEMA, Intermap considers multiple flood models in its assessment and does not acknowledge levees, pump systems and other risk mitigation efforts. It assumes that levees and other systems can fail.
