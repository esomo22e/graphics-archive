The 2016 Gender Gap Could Be The Biggest In Modern Memory

Since 1980, exit polls have shown women voting more Democratically than men. However, this year, the gap could be the biggest it’s been in more than 50 years. Polls right now suggest a 25-point gap between women’s and men’s voting preferences, with women voting around 13 points more Democratic than Republican, and men voting 12 points more Republican than Democratic.

Notes

— Data for 2016 are from three recent national polls, averaged together: CBS/NY Times, IBD/TIPP, and ABC/Washington Post.
— Data through 1972 are based on Gallup’s final pre-election polls.

Source: Gallup (1952 through 1972), Roper Center (1976 through 2012), Recent polls from ABC/Washington Post, IBD/TIPP, CBS/NY Times
