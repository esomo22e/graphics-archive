How The Pope Makes Both U.S. Parties Uncomfortable

TKTKTKTKT

Topic Clashes With What The Pope Has Said What Politicians Have Said The Takeaway
Abortion Democrats “[C]oncern for the protection of nature is also incompatible with the justification of abortion. How can we genuinely teach the importance of concern for other vulnerable beings, however troublesome or inconvenient they may be, if we fail to protect a human embryo, even when its presence is uncomfortable and creates difficulties?” -- Laudato Si "The Democratic Party strongly and unequivocally supports Roe v. Wade and a woman's right to make decisions regarding her pregnancy, including a safe and legal abortion, regardless of ability to pay. We oppose any and all efforts to weaken or undermine that right." -- Democratic Party platform Democrats remain the party of abortion rights. The Catholic Church, meanwhile, is still firmly opposed to abortion.
Abortion Republicans "... I have decided, notwithstanding anything to the contrary, to concede to all priests for the Jubilee Year the discretion to absolve of the sin of abortion those who have procured it and who, with contrite heart, seek forgiveness for it." Pope Francis also commented that, "I am well aware of the pressure that has led [women] to this decision. I know that this is an existential and moral ordeal." -- Sept. 1, 2015 letter "We affirm the dignity of women by protecting the sanctity of human life...We call on the government to permanently ban all federal funding and subsidies for abortion and healthcare plans that include abortion coverage." GOP party platform The Catholic Church is still against abortion, but this (slight) softening of its stance could be considered a blow to pro-life advocates.
Civil Unions Republicans "Marriage is between a man and a woman. ... We have to look at different cases and evaluate them in their variety." -- March 2014 interview (via CNN) "The Republican platform committee resoundingly rejected an amendment ... that would have endorsed civil unions for gay couples." -- Politico, August 21, 2012 The pope's statement here was taken as an indication he might be in favor of civil unions, if not same-sex marriage. The GOP, meanwhile, declined to include the topic in its latest platform.
Climate Change Most Republicans "Society, through non-governmental organizations and intermediate groups, must put pressure on governments to develop more rigorous regulations, procedures and controls." -- Laudato Si "Experience has shown that, in caring for the land and water, private ownership has been our best guarantee of conscientious stewardship, while the worst instances of environmental degradation have occurred under government control." -- Republican Party platform The pope has called on governments to take greater steps on climate change and not to let short-term economic growth cloud their judgment. The GOP platform calls for conservation but emphasizes the role of the private sector and citizens while "reining in the EPA."
Contraception Democrats "Sorry, some people think that in order to be good Catholics we have to breed like rabbits, right? Responsible parenthood: this is why there are marriage support groups in the Church with people who are experts on such issues; and there are pastors and I know that there are many licit ways out that have helped this." -- January 2015 interview with journalists "The President and the Democratic Party believe that women have a right to control their reproductive choices. Democrats support access to affordable family planning services ... . The Affordable Care Act ensures that women have access to contraception in their health insurance plans ... " -- Democratic Party platform The pope's comments on limiting childbearing were seen as endorsement of natural family planning, not artificial contraception (which the church opposes).
Economics Republicans “... [S]ome people continue to defend trickle-down theories which assume that economic growth, encouraged by a free market, will inevitably succeed in bringing about greater justice and inclusiveness in the world. This opinion, which has never been confirmed by the facts, expresses a crude and naïve trust in the goodness of those wielding economic power and in the sacralized workings of the prevailing economic system.” -- Evangelii Gaudium "We offer our Republican vision of a free people using their God-given talents, combined with hard work, self-reliance, ethical conduct, and the pursuit of opportunity, to achieve great things for themselves and the greater community." -- GOP 2016 platform The GOP platform endorses the free-market principles of deregulation and lower taxes as a path to job creation.
Evolution Some Republicans “When we read in Genesis the account of Creation, we risk imagining that God was a magician, with such a magic wand as to be able to do everything. However, it was not like that. ... Evolution in nature is not inconsistent with the notion of creation, because evolution requires the creation of beings that evolve.” Pope Francis' Address at Inauguration of Bronze Bust of Benedict XVI Several states have opened the door to teaching creationism or intelligent design in schools -- and all efforts were led by GOP lawmakers. Slate has a good map of schools where tax dollars pay for these classes. Evolution is not mentioned in either party's platform, but GOP lawmakers have been at the forefront of efforts to include alternative theories, like creationism and intelligent design, in school curricula.
Iran Mostly Republicans Vatican Spokesman Federico Lombardi said, “The agreement on the Iranian nuclear program is viewed in a positive light by the Holy See. It constitutes an important outcome of the negotiations carried out so far, although continued efforts and commitment on the part of all involved will be necessary in order for it to bear fruit.” RNC Chairman Reince Preibus released a statement following the announcement of the Iran deal, saying, "The Obama administration did not even meet its own goals with this deal. If implemented, this agreement will cement the Obama-Clinton foreign policy legacy: a richer, more powerful, more dangerous, legitimized Iran." Opposition to Obama's Iran deal was mostly concentrated among Republicans, though a few Democrats also joined in opposing it.
Palestinian Statehood Republicans The Vatican in May 2015 announced a treaty brokered with "the state of Palestine." This signaled the Vatican's first legal shift from recognizing the Palestinian Liberation Organization to the state of Palestine. “[W]e envision two democratic states – Israel with Jerusalem as its capital and Palestine – living in peace and security. For that to happen, the Palestinian people must support leaders who reject terror, embrace the institutions and ethos of democracy, and respect the rule of law.” GOP party platform The GOP is in favor of a Palestinian state, but only after Palestine meets certain conditions. The Vatican, meanwhile, has already referred to Palestine as a state.
Same-Sex Marriage Some Republicans "If someone is gay and is searching for the Lord and has good will, then who am I to judge him?" -- July 2013 Q&A "We reaffirm our support for a Constitutional amendment defining marriage as the union of one man and one woman. We applaud the citizens of the majority of States which have enshrined in their constitutions the traditional concept of marriage, and we support the campaigns underway in several other States to do so." -- Republican Party platform The church's stance on defining marriage as a union between one man and one woman is unlikely to change any time soon. However, some see his comments as moving away from the church's traditional rhetoric on homosexuals and indiciative of a shifting tone of the church.
U.S.-Cuba Relations Some Republicans "For some months now, we have witnessed an event which fills us with hope: the process of normalizing relations between two peoples following years of estrangement. … I urge political leaders to persevere on this path and to develop all its potentialities as a proof of the high service which they are called to carry out on behalf of the peace and well-being of their peoples, of all America, and as an example of reconciliation for the entire world." -- Sept. 19 statement "We reject any dynastic succession of power within the Castro family and affirm the principles codified in U.S. law as conditions for the lifting of trade, travel, and financial sanctions: the legalization of political parties, an independent media, and free and fair internationally-supervised elections." -- Republican Party platform Democratic politicians are by far more supportive of reestablishing diplomatic relations with Cuba than Republicans are, and that carries over to the general public as well.

Notes

* This was taken as an endorsement of natural family planning (which the church endorses), not of artificial contraception (which the church opposes).
